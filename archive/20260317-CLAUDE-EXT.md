## THE THREE PHILOSOPHICAL PILLARS

These aren't marketing angles — they are the foundation Rieko grew up with in Japan and embedded into the AUWA stories from the very beginning.

### 1. Yaoyorozu no Kami — The Interconnectedness of All Things
Shinto concept that spirits (kami) inhabit all things. Mountains, rivers, trees, animals, and even man-made objects possess a spirit. No sharp divide between sacred and ordinary. Everything is interconnected through a shared divinity. This is the heart of AUWA — the light shower reveals the kokoro within ALL living things.

### 2. Kido Airaku (喜怒哀楽) — The Four Fundamental Emotions
Centuries-old Buddhist/Confucian framework. Four core emotions that are real, historical, and established:
- **喜 (Ki) — Joy:** The Expanding Light (colour: warm gold/amber)
- **怒 (Do) — Anger:** The Rising Heat (colour: deep red/amber)
- **哀 (Ai) — Sorrow:** The Receding Tide (colour: soft blue-grey)
- **楽 (Raku) — Ease:** The Stable Horizon (colour: gentle green/lavender)

Beneath each core emotion sit nuanced **sub-expressions** — poetic labels drawn from nature metaphors (e.g., "Pressed Leaf" for nostalgia, "Coiled Spring" for anticipation, "Desert Well" for loneliness). These operate **under the hood** — they guide the AI's reflections without requiring users to learn a taxonomy. Users experience 4 clear emotions; the depth is felt, not studied.

**Critical decision:** The "72 emotions" concept has been retired. The number was reverse-engineered and risked confusing users about what's real Japanese philosophy vs. what's invented. Kido Airaku (4 emotions) is the user-facing framework. Sub-expressions are AUWA's vocabulary, not the user's taxonomy.

### 3. The 72 Micro-Seasons (Shichijūni-kō) — Constant Change as Philosophy
Ancient Japanese calendar dividing the year into 72 five-day periods. Used as **ambient philosophy**, not user-facing taxonomy. The message: life moves through constant micro-changes, and so do you. That's natural and worthy of observation, not judgment.

In the app: a subtle calligraphic kō stamp on the revelation screen (atmospheric, tappable for the curious). The AI's reflections do NOT force connections to the current micro-season — that mapping would feel artificial (e.g., "rain water" season for someone in a drought). Seasons are the rhythm; emotions are the experience.

## HOW AUWA BUILDS EQ (Four Pillars — Salovey & Mayer)

### Pillar 1: Perceiving Emotions — The Kokoro Revelation
User shares how they feel → AUWA identifies the Kido Airaku emotion + specific sub-expression → reflects it back poetically. Helps users perceive their emotional state more precisely.

**When input is vague** (e.g., "I feel crap"): AUWA acknowledges the feeling and offers a gentle refinement — "Your kokoro feels heavy. Is this the weight of something lost, something blocked, or something you're carrying for too long?" User taps or types more. This IS emotional perception practice.

### Pillar 2: Using Emotions — The Journal Prompt
After revelation, optional journal entry. **Smart pre-population:** if the user's original input was rich ("I feel awful because of that photo of our old flat"), AUWA suggests a brief journal entry drawn from what they already shared ("Saw a photo of the old flat. Memories surfaced."). One tap to save. If input was sparse, the prompt is open.

### Pillar 3: Understanding Emotions — The Kokoro Archive
Visual timeline of cards in 4 Kido Airaku colours (gold, red, blue, purple). At a glance: patterns over days and weeks. Tap to expand: full revelation + journal entry. After 10+ revelations: simple observations ("Sorrow has been most present. The last 3 visits shifted toward Ease."). Observations, not advice.

**4 colours makes patterns instantly visible.** If these were 72 different visual states, the timeline would be noise. Four gives signal.

### Pillar 4: Managing Emotions — Natural Result + Future Partnerships
AUWA doesn't prescribe. Research shows Pillars 1–3 naturally improve regulation. Future: gently surface pathways ("Others who explore Sorrow often find these helpful") — open doors, not pushed through. B2B opportunity: institutions provide their own Pillar 4 resources via white-label platform.

## KOKORO MIRROR APP — UX FLOW (auwa.life)

### Step-by-Step Experience

1. **Arrival:** Dark, calm screen. Soft glowing orb. "AUWA senses a presence. You." Single text input: "How are you feeling right now?" (200-word limit)
2. **Input:** User types freely. No structure, no multiple choice. Submit. Text fades.
3. **Light Shower:** Aurora-coloured particles cascade. 3–5 seconds. Subtle audio on user interaction.
4. **Kokoro Revelation:** Three visual elements:
   - *Gradient background* — 1 of 4 Kido Airaku colour families (shade shifts with sub-expression)
   - *AUWA character variant* — 1 of 4 hand-illustrated by Rieko (Joy, Anger, Sorrow, Ease)
   - *Calligraphic kō stamp* — current micro-season name in Japanese (atmospheric, tappable)
   - Below: AI-generated poetic reflection naming the emotion + sub-expression. **Unique every time** — two users landing on "Pressed Leaf" get different reflections because AUWA responds to their specific words.
5. **Vague Input Refinement:** If input was broad, AUWA offers 2–3 options to tap before delivering full revelation.
6. **Journal Prompt:** Smart pre-populated suggestion (if input was rich) or open prompt. Optional. One tap to save.
7. **Share:** Beautiful card generated (character + gradient + sub-expression name + stamp). Instagram Stories (9:16) and square formats. One tap save to camera roll. Journal stays private.
8. **Save:** Auto-saved to Kokoro Archive. "Your kokoro has been witnessed."
9. **Returning Users:** "AUWA remembers you. Your last visit, you carried [emotion]."

### What Users NEVER See
- The number 72 in relation to emotions
- Micro-season names forced into reflection text
- Any taxonomy or framework diagram
- Advice, prescriptions, or "try this" suggestions

### Freemium Model (Updated)
- **Free tier:** Unlimited core revelations, journal, Kokoro Archive, shareable cards
- **Paid tier (£6.99/month or £49.99/year):** AI memory across sessions, activity-emotion correlation insights, extended archive analytics (month/quarter/year views, Year in Kokoro visualisation), story unlocks, early access to figure drops
- Rationale: free tier builds the habit and community; paid tier unlocks depth, not access

## ILLUSTRATION STRATEGY (Critical Asset)

### Core Principle
The illustrations are the foundation everything else builds on. They unlock every workstream: social content, app visual identity, digital stories, Runway animations, figure design, press material, shareable kokoro cards.

**Rieko's original art is the source of truth.** AI (MidJourney) refreshes and scales her vision for digital formats — it doesn't replace it. Rieko is the artist. AI is the production team.

### Illustration Refresh Process
- 150 illustrations across 4 books need refreshing for digital-first release
- Style locked using MidJourney Style Creator with Rieko's originals as primary style references, influenced by: Matthew Forsythe (Aggie and the Ghost), Oliver Jeffers (Here We Are), Jon Klassen (The Skull, The Dark), Issa Watanabe (Migrants)
- MidJourney setup: Image Prompts (composition) + Style References (aesthetic) + Omni Reference (character lock)
- Timeline: ~10–12 working days (style lock 2 days, batch generation 3 days, Rieko curation + refinement 4–5 days, batch resize half day)
- Rieko slowly hand-redraws key scenes over time for timelapse videos (authenticity proof)

### Authenticity Protocol
- Always credit Rieko as creator: "Illustrated by Rieko Vining, produced with AI tools"
- Social mix: polished AI-assisted seasonal cards + Rieko's hand-drawing process, original sketches, physical manuscripts
- The human fingerprint must always be visible
- Original manuscripts preserved as archive, exhibition material, and proof of decade-long development

### Crossover Audience
AUWA's illustration style (looks like "children's book") reaches adults precisely because it disarms and bypasses cynicism. Precedents: Charlie Mackesy (The Boy, the Mole, the Fox and the Horse — 7M copies, Oscar-winning film, adult audience), Oliver Jeffers, Studio Ghibli, Moomin (Tove Jansson). The format is a Trojan horse for philosophy.

## COMPETITIVE POSITIONING

### Category: "Daily EQ Practice" (New Category)
AUWA doesn't compete in existing categories. Closest competitors are AI journaling apps (Stoic, Rosebud, Mindsera, Reflection.app). None combine: cultural philosophy + companion character + visual art system + shareable output + physical collectibles + four-pillar EQ framework.

### The Authenticity Moat
Features can be copied. Cultural authenticity cannot. Rieko's decade of development, native Japanese grounding, 4 illustrated books, Yaoyorozu no Kami philosophy embedded by lived experience — these cannot be prompted, researched, or bought.

### Key Competitors
- **Stoic** (most relevant) — philosophy-grounded daily practice, Western tradition, no AI personalisation, no visual art
- **Rosebud** — AI emotional reflection, therapeutic chatbot approach, no cultural depth
- **Mindsera** — philosophy + AI, Western analytical frameworks, complex
- **Daylio** — frictionless mood tracking, no depth
- **Headspace/Calm** — stress reduction, not EQ building
- **Labubu/KAWS** — collectible figures, no narrative depth or digital companion

## KEY PRINCIPLES & DECISIONS

### What AUWA Is
- A daily EQ practice, not a meditation app
- A mirror that reveals, not a coach that fixes
- Grounded in real Japanese philosophy (Yaoyorozu no Kami, Kido Airaku), not invented frameworks
- Beautiful, poetic, and shareable
- Authentic — Rieko's hand is always visible

### What AUWA Is Not
- Not clinical (no CBT, no diagnosis, no coping strategies)
- Not gamified (no streaks, no badges, no push notifications)
- Not generic (not another AI chatbot with a wellness skin)
- Not a weather app (micro-seasons are philosophy, not forecasts)
- Not advice-giving (AUWA reveals, period)

### Key Strategic Decisions Made
- 4 Kido Airaku emotions user-facing, sub-expressions under the hood (not 72 emotions)
- Micro-seasons as ambient philosophy, NOT tied to AI reflection text
- Web app (PWA) first, wrappable for native later via Capacitor
- Free tier is unlimited core experience; paid tier unlocks depth, not access
- Illustrations are the most valuable asset — refresh them BEFORE building the app
- No vanilla/character-free version (the character IS the differentiator)
- No physical journal until app journal usage proves demand
- Hotel B2B deprioritised to Year 2 discovery conversations
- All More Air client revenue separated from AUWA financials

### The Hierarchy of Value (Most to Least Durable)
1. **Permanent, uncopyable:** Rieko's original art + manuscripts, the 4 stories + AUWA mythology, Yaoyorozu no Kami + Kido Airaku foundation, Rieko's identity as Japanese creator from Kansai, combined backstory
2. **Valuable, adaptable:** Refreshed illustration library, character designs, seasonal content system, brand identity, social following
3. **Useful today, potentially commoditised tomorrow:** The app itself, the tech stack, the AI reflection engine, the payment integration
