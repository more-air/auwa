## THE AUWA UNIVERSE (LORE)

### Origin Story

AUWA originates from WAWA, a harmonious solar system deep within the Milky Way, consisting of six vibrant planets orbiting a radiant yellow-green sun. The inhabitants live through love, unity, and telepathic communication. When they received a mysterious signal from Earth—a planet teetering on imbalance—they channelled energy from each planet and the sun to form a glowing orb of pure light. From this orb emerged AUWA.

**AUWA's Power:** The magical light shower
- When released, illuminates the kokoro of every living creature it touches
- Reveals hidden emotions, thoughts, and the soul's essence
- Non-judgmental, observant, gentle

**AUWA's Communication:** Entirely telepathic
- No spoken words
- Poetic, reflective, philosophical
- **AUWA does not fix. AUWA reveals.**

### The 72 Emotions Framework

**Based on Japan's 72 Micro-Seasons (Shichijūni-kō):**
- Ancient calendar dividing the year into 72 five-day periods
- Each period reflects subtle natural changes in the natural world
- Each period tied to a distinct emotional state
- Perpetual content engine — changes every 5 days
- Rooted in Kido Airaku (Japanese emotional philosophy, 400+ years old)

**Example Emotions:**
- Wistfulness (物思い - monotsui): Gentle melancholy of remembering, honoured not avoided
- Serenity (静寂 - seijaku): Peaceful stillness
- Yugen (幽玄): Mysterious profundity
- Aware (哀れ): Gentle sadness, poignant beauty
- Natsukashii (懐かしい): Nostalgic longing

**Framework Purpose:**
- Expands emotional vocabulary (naming emotions = first step to understanding them)
- Ties emotions to natural cycles (seasonal grounding builds empathy for external rhythms)
- Provides cultural depth (Japanese philosophy 400+ years in development)
- Creates collectible structure (72 to explore, collect, and embody)
- Builds EQ through perspective-taking: *"In Usui (Rain Water), the earth absorbs moisture slowly. Your Wistfulness is like this—a slow saturation, not a storm."*

### Illustrated Books (2015–2025)

Four completed, unpublished books forming the archival IP foundation:

- **The Beginning** — AUWA's creation and arrival on Earth
- **Umi** — Ocean creatures and their kokoro
- **Human** — Human emotions and connections

**Development Timeline:**
- Conceived: 2015 | Developed: 2015–2025 (10 years) | Created by: Rieko Vining

*Why collectors care:* Mass production kills value (the Funko Pop problem). Scarcity creates demand. Numbered editions trigger investment mentality.

*How to present:* *"Every AUWA figure is a limited edition. We will never mass-produce. Each run is 50–100 pieces, hand-finished, numbered, and signed by the artist. When they're gone, they're gone forever. No reprints."*

*Why collectors care:* Hand-finished = soul. Collectors feel the human touch. Imperfections = proof of authenticity. Factory-perfect = soulless (the Funko Pop problem again).

*How to present:* *"Each AUWA figure is hand-finished using traditional techniques. No two are identical. You're not buying mass-produced vinyl—you're buying a miniature artwork."*

*Collector psychology:* "This isn't a toy, it's an art object" / "Hand-finished = the artist touched this" / "Imperfections make mine unique"

**5. "Narrative Depth (Not Just a Cute Character)"**

*Why collectors care:* Story = emotional connection = long-term attachment. Lore = conversation piece. Cute character with no backstory = disposable trend.

*How to present:* *"AUWA is a cosmic being from WAWA who reveals the kokoro (soul) within all living things through a magical light shower. **6. "Artist-Signed, Not Factory-Stamped"**

*Why collectors care:* Artist signature = direct connection to creator. Signed = investment-grade (like fine art). Unsigned = just a product.

*Execution:* Signature on base of figure (permanent marker or engraved); certificate hand-signed in ink (not printed); Rieko may add a small sketch on certificate

**7. "No Hype, Just Quality"**

*Why collectors care:* "BUY NOW LIMITED HYPE DROP" brands = red flag (cash grab). Quiet confidence = serious creators. KAWS didn't hype — the work spoke for itself.
