# AUWA Arrival

*Created: April 2026. The strategy for how AUWA enters the world. Maintained as living document.*
*Load when discussing launch, promotion, channel strategy, budget allocation, or the next 12 months.*

---

## Why "Arrival" and Not "Launch"

AUWA doesn't launch. The four pillars ship over 18-24 months: the Journal is already live; the App arrives in Q4 2026; the Store in Year 2; the Book whenever we decide. There is no single day when something clicks live and metrics spike. What we're doing is establishing a cadence, not triggering an event. Call it arrival. The brand simply shows up, reliably, and compounds.

"Launch" also carries the wrong emotional register for the Gift Ethos. Launches announce. Arrivals are welcomed. The difference is the posture.

---

## The Frame

Three things make AUWA different from a standard product launch.

**This is a brand launch, not a product launch.** Only the Journal is commercially live. "Launch" really means the 12-18 months during which we establish a cultural position, build an audience, and prepare to sell. Expecting a spike is the wrong expectation. Compounding is the right one.

**We're building a long-duration thing from a low-intensity life.** A three-to-four-day week doesn't support a six-week launch sprint. The cadence must be sustainable indefinitely, not a campaign that ends. This rules out most of what startups default to: daily posting, PR blitzes, constant webinars. What works for AUWA is the opposite. Small consistent moves every week, for a long time, that accumulate.

**We're optimising for the right 1,000 people, not 50,000.** AUWA's audience is narrow by design: design-conscious, culturally curious, affluent, suspicious of hype. Every growth decision should be tested against the question: *"does this attract the right person, or does this attract noise?"*

## The Four Phases

Four phases, roughly three months each. Soft boundaries, not hard ones.

### Phase 1: Quiet Debut (April-June 2026)

Website goes live publicly but quietly. Instagram restarts. No announcements, no LinkedIn post, no press outreach. The brand exists and people find it. Email list grows from the warm network outward. Articles ship at two per month, reliably. This is where we earn the right to be louder later by doing the unglamorous work of shipping consistently.

**Target by end of June:**
- 1,500-2,000 IG followers
- 250-400 email subscribers
- 20 published journal articles

### Phase 2: Selective Amplification (July-September 2026)

With three months of consistent output behind us, activate the warm network in sequence:

1. **CMO friend first** (his job is to critique the strategy before you amplify it)
2. **Ivo Weevers second** (launch sequencing input, he's done this before)
3. **LinkedIn post by Tom** mid-phase, pointing peers to the journal first, app second
4. **Fin DAC collab** in late phase (he's already warm, 96K followers)
5. **Guest articles** pitched to aligned publications (pick two, not all four)

Still no traditional press.

**Target by end of September:**
- 3,000-3,500 followers
- 800 subscribers
- One or two guest pieces live
- First collab landed

### Phase 3: App Soft Launch (October-December 2026)

Kokoro Mirror arrives. The first commercial moment. Announce in character: *"Something small we've made."* Free tier leads, paid tier present but not loud. £2K paid ad budget begins, metered across 90 days. First press outreach begins, targeting publications that covered Kinfolk, Hodinkee, Stoic, Calm. Book fragments start appearing on IG as editorial. Store craftsman outreach continues in the background, invisible publicly.

**Target by end of December:**
- 5,000 followers (the gate we've been building toward)
- 2,000 subscribers
- 500+ app users
- Two or three press pieces landed

### Phase 4: Store Prep + Book Decision (January-March 2027)

Craftsman partnerships firm up. Book release decision made (see forks below). App V2 planned or released. First exhibition or gallery approach if we want that moment. Year 1 strategic review.

## Channel Strategy

We don't do "everything everywhere." We do a few things deeply and let them compound.

**Instagram: primary.** Full detail in `instagram.md`. Three posts per week sustainably. Gift Ethos. Character content at 35% weight. Editorial slideshows from articles. Atmospheric weekend posts. This is where the audience is built.

**LinkedIn: peer amplification, not a content channel.** The AUWA LinkedIn company page exists (use the tagline, description, and imagery locked this week). Its role is to make the brand legible to industry peers when Tom posts personally in Phase 2. Occasionally repost a published journal article or press mention. Not a daily posting platform. The AUWA LinkedIn page is a storefront, not a megaphone.

Tom's personal LinkedIn post in Phase 2 is a separate moment: a brief note sharing AUWA with his 1K+ peers, pointing them to the journal first (not to the app teaser). Frame it as sharing craft work, not asking for support.

**Awwwards and design recognition sites.** Submit the website once it's been live and stable for 4-6 weeks and has 15+ articles. Most are free to submit to; paid tiers are optional. Even a "Nominee" mention drives backlinks and taste-audience visibility. Targets:
- Awwwards (free submission; paid £150ish for Pro)
- SiteInspire
- CSS Design Awards
- Designspiration
- Typewolf
- httpster
- Minimal.gallery
- Godly.website (for brands with spiritual/philosophical leaning)

Not all will accept. Submit to the top four; the others are if-you-have-time.

**Guest articles: Phase 2.** Pitch one or two articles to aligned publications. Don't fire-and-forget to ten at once. Write each pitch like a journal article: specific, voice-aligned, generous with the idea. Publications worth considering:
- Kinfolk
- Monocle
- Hodinkee
- It's Nice That
- Cereal
- Aeon (for deeper philosophical pieces)
- The Marginalian (long-shot but aligned)
- Apollo Magazine

Pick two that feel most natural and commit. One landed piece outperforms five pitched and ignored.

**Organic link-building: Phase 2-3.** Make a list of 15-20 aligned brands and blogs. Email editors personally, not as outreach. Introduce AUWA quietly, link to one specific article they might genuinely love. Expect a 10-20% response rate. Backlinks from aligned sites compound SEO over 6-12 months. Candidates:
- Nalata Nalata
- Analogue Life
- Rikumo
- Tortoise General Store
- Postalco
- Kinto
- Zakkaya
- Japan-focused travel/design blogs
- Seasonal living accounts on Substack

**Press: Phase 3 onward.** Target freelancers who've written for Wallpaper*, Monocle, Wired, Fast Company, Vogue Business, Dezeen, and the design/wellness beats of broader publications. Research each writer's recent pieces before pitching. A single well-placed piece at the right moment (app soft launch) outperforms ten generic press releases.

**Newsletter: passive in Phase 1-2, active in Phase 3.** Grows organically from website footer and IG bio throughout Phase 1-2. In Phase 3, add small paid IG ads targeting direct newsletter signups (lead form ads perform well; no landing page friction).

**Email outreach to niche communities.** Japanese culture societies, craft collectors, design schools, mindful/wellness groups. Very targeted, very small. Not mass outreach. Could drive 50-100 quality subscribers if done well in Phase 2.

**Podcast appearances: post-5K only.** Pitch after Phase 3 when we have social proof. Start list of aligned shows now; reach out later.

**What we don't do:**
- TikTok (post-5K, when we have Reel winners to repurpose)
- YouTube (post-5K, as Phase 2+ depth content)
- Twitter/X (wrong audience, wrong atmosphere)
- Pinterest (maybe later, not a priority)
- Threads (wait and see)
- Facebook page (required for Meta ad buying only; not a content channel)

## Budget: £2-3K Across 12 Months

Suggested allocation of a £2,500 budget:

**Instagram paid ads: £1,800-2,000.** The primary growth lever. As detailed in `instagram.md`: metered £15-25/day across Phase 3 (October-December). Split within that budget: 60% engagement/awareness (follower growth), 30% traffic to newsletter/teaser pages, 10% retargeting. Native-feeling Reel ads outperform polished "ad-looking" creative.

**Design award submissions: £150-300.** Awwwards Pro submission, CSS Design Awards fee, etc. Apply only where the recognition would meaningfully compound (Awwwards does; some others don't). Recognition compounds SEO and peer respect; avoid paying for trophies without downstream value.

**Newsletter growth ads: £200-300.** Small, focused IG campaigns in Phase 2-3 pointing to newsletter signup (lead form ads). Goal is subscriber quality, not quantity.

**Contingency: £200-400.** Press outreach tools (Hunter.io for email finding, £30/mo for 2 months), unexpected opportunities, a small push around a specific moment if one materialises.

**What we don't spend on:**
- Google Ads (wrong audience, wrong intent, wasted spend)
- Facebook ads separately from Instagram (same Meta network; manage from one place)
- Influencer marketing (Fin DAC is warm; never pay influencers)
- PR agency (our voice is the asset; an agency dilutes it)
- Retargeting before we have traffic (pointless in Phase 1)
- Boosting posts (always-on boosts perform worse than structured campaigns)

**Budget principle:** most of the marketing value comes from consistent content and thoughtful outreach, both free. Paid spend amplifies what's working, doesn't create traction where there's none. Don't spend in Phase 1. Save the budget for Phase 3 when there's something to amplify.

## The Three Forks

Decisions to make deliberately, not slide into.

**Fork 1: App or Store first.** Current answer: **app**. The app is closer to ready (no craftsman-sourcing bottleneck), it's the cleaner "quiet debut" product, and it's easier to iterate based on real usage. Worth confirming this in writing so the next three months of build work are unambiguous. If Rieko's craftsman conversations suddenly accelerate (one or two masters committing this month), the calculus could shift. Absent that, app first.

**Fork 2: Book release timing.** Three options:

(a) **Hold indefinitely as keystone asset** until 10K+ followers. Maximum impact when released, but risks never feeling ready.

(b) **Digital version bundled with paid app tier** in Phase 3. Uses the book as a premium-tier hook. Clever but slightly reduces the book's standalone weight.

(c) **Full print run in Phase 4** as its own launch moment. The book is physically beautiful and deserves its own stage. Print run decision becomes capital allocation (£3-8K for a short run).

Current lean: (c). The book deserves its own moment. Decision needs making in Phase 2 so print production timeline lines up with Phase 4 release.

**Fork 3: Character exclusivity.** Currently AUWA the character lives in the book and app only, not the journal or store. Do we hold that line, or let the character bleed into IG more broadly?

Current lean: **hold the line**. Scarcity across pillars is the character's value. IG character posts are fine because they're promotional; they don't dilute the character's presence in the pillars themselves. But don't put AUWA on journal articles, on store product pages, or on the homepage beyond the hero video. This is a taste call, not a strategic one, and Rieko should have the final say.
