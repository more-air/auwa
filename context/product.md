## KOKORO MIRROR — DETAILED UX FLOW

1. **Arrival:** Dark, calm screen. Soft glowing orb. Single text input: "How are you feeling right now?" (200-word limit).
2. **Input:** User types freely. No structure, no multiple choice. Submit. Text fades.
3. **Light Shower:** Aurora-coloured particles cascade, 3-5 seconds. Subtle audio on interaction.
4. **Kokoro Revelation:** Gradient background (1 of 4 Kido Airaku colour families, shade shifts with sub-expression) + AUWA character variant (1 of 4, hand-illustrated by Rieko) + calligraphic ko stamp (current micro-season, atmospheric, tappable) + AI-generated poetic reflection. Unique every time.
5. **Vague Input Refinement:** If broad, AUWA offers 2-3 options before full revelation. "Your kokoro feels heavy. Is this the weight of something lost, something blocked, or something you're carrying for too long?"
6. **Journal Prompt:** Smart pre-populated suggestion (if input was rich) or open prompt. Optional. One tap to save.
7. **Share:** Card generated (character + gradient + sub-expression + stamp). 9:16 and square formats. One tap save to camera roll. Journal stays private.
8. **Save:** Auto-saved to Kokoro Archive. "Your kokoro has been witnessed."
9. **Returning Users:** "AUWA remembers you. Your last visit, you carried [emotion]."

### What Users Never See
The number 72 in relation to emotions. Micro-season names forced into reflection text. Any taxonomy or framework diagram. Advice, prescriptions, or "try this" suggestions.

## EQ BUILDING (SALOVEY & MAYER FOUR PILLARS)

**Pillar 1 (Perceiving):** Kokoro revelation identifies emotion + sub-expression, reflects it back poetically. Helps users perceive their emotional state more precisely.

**Pillar 2 (Using):** Journal prompt after revelation. Smart pre-population from what the user already shared. One tap to save.

**Pillar 3 (Understanding):** Kokoro Archive as visual timeline in 4 colours. Patterns visible at a glance. After 10+ revelations: observations, not advice ("Sorrow has been most present. The last 3 visits shifted toward Ease.").

**Pillar 4 (Managing):** Natural result of Pillars 1-3. Future: gently surface pathways. B2B opportunity for institutions to provide their own Pillar 4 resources.

## COLLECTIBLE FIGURES

Small batch, 3D-printed in-house on BambuLab X1 Carbon. Hand-finished, numbered, signed by Rieko. Tied to Kido Airaku emotions. £120-180. Pre-order model.

**First release (Month 3-4):** 50-100 units, single emotion (Wistfulness or Serenity). Certificate of authenticity + story booklet.

**Rules:** Never more than 100 units per emotion. Never reissue sold-out figures (scarcity is sacred). Variant colourways: 25-50 units, released 3 months after original.

### Collector Positioning (Seven Principles)
1. 10-year development before first release (depth, not cash grab)
2. Japanese artist + cultural authenticity (not Western brand doing kawaii)
3. Limited editions forever (no mass production)
4. Craftsmanship over manufacturing (hand-finished, imperfections = proof)
5. Narrative depth (character with decade-long mythology)
6. Artist-signed, not factory-stamped
7. No hype, just quality (calm, thoughtful tone, never "ONLY 3 LEFT")

### Never Mention
Investment potential (let secondary market prove it). Comparisons to KAWS/Labubu (sounds derivative). Urgency tactics. Cheap versions (kills premium positioning permanently).
