--- CAPTION (paste this whole block as-is into the IG caption field) ---

The old Japanese calendar divides the year into seventy-two seasons (shichijuni-kō), each one five days long. Each name describes something specific you could verify by walking outside: the first thunder, the plum blossoms opening, hawks learning to fly. A way of treating the year as something happening around you, now.
