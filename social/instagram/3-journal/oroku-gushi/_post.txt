The minebari tree, called the tree that breaks axes in Japanese, is dried for thirteen years before a craftsman touches it. From it, master artisans in the Kiso Valley carve oroku-gushi, fine-toothed combs with a hundred teeth packed into eight centimetres. Used daily, they darken with the natural oils of your hair, becoming your own.
